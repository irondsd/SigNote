# Secrets 🔒

Secrets are the second tier of privacy in SigNote. The note body is encrypted in your browser before it ever leaves your device. The server stores only ciphertext — it cannot read what you've written.

---

## How Secrets work

When you save a Secret, the body is encrypted client-side using AES-GCM 256-bit encryption before being sent to the server. Only the **title** is stored as plaintext (to support search and navigation).

When you open the Secrets section, all bodies are decrypted in the background for your session — so browsing feels fast once you've unlocked.

---

## Unlock flow 🔑

To access your Secrets, you need to unlock encryption with your passphrase. Here's what happens:

1. You enter your passphrase
2. The app re-derives your `deviceShare` using PBKDF2 (600,000 iterations)
3. It fetches your `serverShare` from the server
4. The **Master Encryption Key (MEK)** is reconstructed: `deviceShare XOR serverShare`
5. A session key (`secretBodyKey`) is derived from the MEK using HKDF
6. All Secret bodies are decrypted with `secretBodyKey` and held in memory for the session

The passphrase itself is never stored anywhere.

---

## Auto-lock 🔓

SigNote has two levels of automatic locking to protect your Secrets when you're not actively using them.

### Soft lock — tab hidden

When you switch away from the tab (or minimise the window), SigNote immediately clears the MEK from memory and hides all decrypted content. Your `deviceShare` is kept in `sessionStorage`.

When you return to the tab and click **Unlock**, the MEK is silently reconstructed from `deviceShare` without asking for your passphrase again. You're back in your session instantly.

### Hard lock — inactivity or sleep

A hard lock occurs when:

- You click the **Lock** button explicitly
- The tab has been inactive for **5 minutes** (no mouse, keyboard, or touch input)
- The device goes to sleep (detected when the tab resumes after a gap of 30+ seconds)

On hard lock:

- The MEK is cleared from memory
- The `deviceShare` is cleared from `sessionStorage`
- All decrypted content is immediately inaccessible

To access your Secrets again, you must enter your passphrase. Closing the tab also clears `deviceShare` automatically, since `sessionStorage` is tab-scoped.

---

## Search

Secrets can be searched by **title only**. Because bodies are encrypted, the server can't index them.

If full-body search is important to you, use [Notes](/docs/notes) instead. If title-only search isn't enough and you need stronger isolation per note, consider [Seals](/docs/seals).

---

## Secrets vs Seals

| | Secrets 🔒 | Seals 🛡️ |
|---|---|---|
| Encryption key | Shared session key | Unique key per note |
| Decryption | All at once on unlock | One at a time, on demand |
| Speed | Fast (bulk decrypt) | Slower (individual decrypt) |
| Blast radius if key leaked | All secrets | Just one note |
| Best for | Passwords, info you access often | Your most sensitive data |

→ See [Seals](/docs/seals) for the strongest privacy tier.

---

## Technical details

- **Algorithm:** AES-GCM 256-bit
- **Key:** `secretBodyKey` — derived from MEK via HKDF with info string `signote-v1/secret-body`
- **IV:** Fresh random 12 bytes per encryption operation
- **Stored:** Ciphertext + IV in MongoDB (no plaintext body ever written server-side)
